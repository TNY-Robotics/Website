# Top Assembly

After making the Bottom of the leg, let's now assemble the Top part !

We'll start by adding the Servo Horn and a servomotor to it.

---

## Add the Round Servo Horn

For this step, you'll need:

| Ressource                     | Quantity |
| ----------------------------- | :------: |
| Round Servo Horn              |    1     |
| `M1.4 x 5mm` screws           |    4     |
| Printed part `Leg-Top (BL)`   |    1     |

### Place the Servo Horn

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/leg-bl/top/servo-horn-place.webm" />

#text
**Insert the Round Servo Horn** into the slot at the top of the `Leg-Top (BL)` part.

Make sure the small slot for the servo axle is **pointing up** and the horn sits flush in the recess.
::

### Screw the Servo Horn

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/leg-bl/top/servo-horn-screw.webm" />

#text
**Align the holes** of the `Leg-Top (BL)` part and the holes of the Round Servo Horn, then secure the horn with the **four `M1.4 x 5mm` screws**.

Tighten the screws until the horn is snug, but **avoid overtightening them** !
::

---

## Add the Servomotor

For this step, you'll need:

| Ressource                                                                     | Quantity |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------: |
| **Left** MG996R Servomotor *(the ones with 30cm long wires and an "L" label)* |    1     |
| `M2.5 x 10mm` Screws *(from the servo bag)*                                   |    4     |

### Place the Servomotor

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/leg-bl/top/servo-place.webm" />

#text
Place the servomotor into the bottom cavity of the `Leg-Top (BL)` part, and check that the **axle is pointing toward the bottom**.

Make sure servomotor sits flush with the `Leg-Top (BL)` part.
::

### Screw the Servomotor

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/leg-bl/top/servo-screw.webm" />

#text
Secure the servomotor using the **four `M2.5 x 10mm` screws**.

As always, tighten the screws until it's snug, but **don't overtighten them to avoid any damage** to the motor or printed part.
::

---

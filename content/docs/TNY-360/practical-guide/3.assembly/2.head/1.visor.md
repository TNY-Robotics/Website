# Assembling the Visor

Let's start by assembling the visor, which is the part that holds most of the sensors, the screen, and the main board.

There's a lot of components here, so take your time to follow the instructions carefully !

---

## Assembly the Visor Plate

We'll first add all the components to the plate going behind the visor (the `Visor-Plate` part), and then we'll fix it to the visor.

For this part, you'll need :

| Ressource               | Quantity |
| ----------------------- | :------: |
| The `Visor-Plate` part  |    1     |
| The `OV2640` Camera     |    1     |
| The `Camera Mount` part |    1     |
| The OLED Screen         |    1     |
| The Laser Sensor        |    1     |
| The Microphone          |    1     |
| Two Touch Sensors       |    2     |

### Place the Camera in the Camera Mount

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/camera-mount.webm" />

#text
First, **insert the camera** in the `Camera Mount`.

Check that the **camera's cable is pointing up** and the camera is fully inserted in the mount.
::

### Add the Camera Mount to the Visor Plate

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/camera-place.webm" />

#text
Next, **push the camera mount through the hole** at the bottom of the `Visor-Plate` part.

Make sure to **Pull the camera cable through the hole** before pushing the mount in it.
::

### Add the Screen to the Visor Plate

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/screen-place.webm" />

#text
Next, **place the OLED screen** on the top of the `Visor-Plate` part.

Check that the **screen's wires are on the top** and the screen is fully seated on the plate. 
::

### Add the Laser Sensor to the Visor Plate

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/laser-place.webm" />

#text
Then, add the laser sensor by **pushing its cable through the hole** on the left of the `Visor-Plate` part,
and then **pressing the sensor on the plate**.
::

### Add the Microphone to the Visor Plate

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/microphone-place.webm" />

#text
Then, add the microphone by **pushing its cable through the hole** on the right of the `Visor-Plate` part,
and then **pressing the microphone on the plate**.
::

### Add the Left Touch Sensor to the Visor Plate

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/touch-left-place.webm" />

#text
Now that the bottom components are in place, we can add the touch sensors on either sides of the screen.

Start by **sliding the left touch sensor on the plate**, making sure that the **cables go through the visor** plate.
::

### Add the Right Touch Sensor to the Visor Plate

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/touch-right-place.webm" />

#text
Now that the left touch sensor is in place, you can add the right one by **sliding it on the plate** as well, making sure that the **cables go through the visor** plate.
::

---

## Fix the Visor Plate to the Visor

The `Visor-Plate` is now filled with all the components, and it's time to fix it to the visor.

For this part, you'll need :

| Ressource              | Quantity |
| ---------------------- | :------: |
| The `Visor-Plate` part |    1     |
| The `Visor` part       |    1     |
| `M2.5x4mm`             |    2     |

### Place the Visor Plate on top of the Visor

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/visor-plate-place.webm" />

#text
Start by **placing the `Visor-Plate` on top of the `Visor`** part.

Keep the components on the `Visor-Plate` during this step, and **make sure that they are all properly aligned** with the holes and cutouts of the `Visor` part.
::

### Screw the Visor Plate

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/visor-plate-screw.webm" />

#text
Now that the `Visor-Plate` is placed on the `Visor` with all the components aligned correctly, you can secure it with two `M2.5x4mm` screws.

Make sure to tighten them until the `Visor-Plate` is snug against the `Visor`, but **avoid overtightening** to prevent damage to the parts.
::

---

## Wire the components to the Main Board

See all these wires coming out of the Visor Plate ? It's time to connect them to the main board !

For this part, you'll need :

| Ressource               | Quantity |
| ----------------------- | :------: |
| The `Main Board` PCB    |    1     |
| 5cm-long 2.4Ghz Antenna |    1     |

### Wire the Camera

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/wire-camera.webm" />

#text
We'll first connect the camera to the main board. To do that, **pull the black tab** on the bottom connector of the main board.

Then, **insert the camera's cable into the connector**, and push the black tab back in place to secure the cable.
::

### Wire the Screen

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/wire-screen.webm" />

#text
Now we'll connect the screen to the main board.

**Plug the screen's connector** in to the JST-PH 4-Pin connector on the main board **with the :logo{name="screen"} Screen logo** next to it.
::

### Wire the Laser Sensor

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/wire-laser.webm" />

#text
It's now time to wire the laser sensor to the main board.

**Connect the laser sensor's connector** to the JST-PH 4-Pin connector on the main board **with the :logo{name="laser"} Laser logo** next to it.
::

### Wire the Microphone

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/wire-microphone.webm" />

#text
We'll now connect the microphone to the main board.

**Plug the microphone's connector** into the JST-PH 5-Pin connector on the main board **with the :logo{name="mic"} Microphone logo** next to it.
::

### Wire the Left Touch Sensor

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/wire-left-touch.webm" />

#text
All good ! We'll now connect the left touch sensor to the main board.

Connect the left touch sensor's connector to the JST-PH 3-Pin connector on the main board **with the :logo{name="button"} Button logo** next to it.
::

### Wire the Right Touch Sensor

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/wire-right-touch.webm" />

#text
Do the same thing for the right touch sensor, connecting it to the other JST-PH 3-Pin connector on the main board **with the :logo{name="button"} Button logo** next to it.
::

### Add the antenna

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/wire-antenna.webm" />

#text
We'll now add the antenna, which is a delicate component, so be gentle with it !

Connect the antenna's connector to the U.FL connector at the center of the main board.

Make sure the antenna is pointing up *(towards the USB-C connector)* and that the cable is not bent or twisted.
::

---

## Install the Main Board

Everything is wired up, now it's fix the board to the visor !

For this step, you'll need :

| Ressource           | Quantity |
| ------------------- | :------: |
| `M2.5 x 4mm` screws |    4     |
| `SG90` servomotors  |    2     |

### Connect the Ear motors

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/main-board-place.webm" />

#text
Before placing the main board, we need to connect the ear motors to it, as it will be harder to do it once the board is screwed in place.

**Connect the left ear motor** to the Left connector at the top-left of the main board. Make sure the **red wire is where the "+" symbol** is on the board.

Then, **connect the right ear motor** to the Right connector at the top-right of the main board, making sure that the red wire is also on the "+" side.
::


### Place the Main Board in the Visor

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/main-board-place.webm" />

#text
Place the main board in the center of the `Visor` part, with the USB-C connector pointing up.

Align the holes of the main board with the holes of the `Visor` part to be ready to screw it in place.
::

### Screw the Main Board

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/main-board-screw.webm" />

#text
Now that the board is properly placed, you can secure it with four `M2.5 x 4mm` screws.

Make sure to tighten the screws until the board is snug against the visor, but **avoid overtightening** to prevent damage to the parts.
::

---

## Fix the antenna

Now that the main board is installed, we'll fix the antenna to the visor to make sure it doesn't move around and that has maximum range.

### Remove the adhesive protection

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/antenna-adhesive.webm" />

#text
The antenna has an adhesive on the back, but it's protected by a plastic film to prevent it from sticking to things before we want it to.

Remove this protective film to expose the adhesive, and be careful not to touch the adhesive with your fingers to keep it clean and sticky.
::

### Place the antenna on the Visor

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/antenna-place.webm" />

#text
Place the antenna on the top of the visor, by gently bending the cable back in the head.

Make sure the antenna is straight and well aligned with the top of the visor to avoid any damage to the cable.
::

### Press the antenna

::step
#animation
<Animation src="/docs/images/assembly/head/visor/antenna-press.webm" />

#text
Now that the antenna is in place, press it firmly against the visor to make sure it sticks well.

Any gap between the visor and the antenna will reduce the antenna's range, so make sure it's well pressed against the visor.
::

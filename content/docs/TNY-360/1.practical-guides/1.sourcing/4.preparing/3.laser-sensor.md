# The Laser sensor

This one's pretty much the same as the screen, since it's also an I2C component :)

---

## The connection

The laser sensor uses the same 4-pin JST-PH connector as the screen, so the connection is the same as well. <br>
We recommend a length of **around 5cm to 10cm** for this cable, to have enough slack to connect the laser easily,
but not too much to avoid having a lot of excess cable inside the head.

Here's a reminder of the pinout of the 4-pin JST-PH connector, as always:

::split{side="right"}
#left
1. **GND**: The ground. *(which is the pin most to the left)*
2. **VCC**: The power supply. *(which is the second pin from the left)*
3. **SDA**: The data line. *(which is the third pin from the left)*
4. **SCL**: The clock line. *(which is the pin most to the right)*

::

## The result

Here's what the connection should look like after soldering the JST-PH connector to the laser sensor:

::split{side="right"}
#left
As for the screen before, the laser sensor pin order is [`VCC`, `GND`, `SDA`, `SCL`],
which is different from the JST-PH connector pin order [`GND`, `VCC`, `SDA`, `SCL`].

So we swapped the GND and VCC wires to match the pinout of the laser sensor.
